# First Principles

A first principle is a basic proposition or assumption that cannot be deduced from any other proposition. In philosophy and science, first principles form the foundation upon which all other knowledge is built.

Aristotle defined a first principle as "the first basis from which a thing is known." These are self-evident truths that require no proof.

See also [[Cogito]] and [[Law of Non-Contradiction]].
